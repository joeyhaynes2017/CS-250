# CS-250

Throughout this course, I’ve learned that understanding user needs really comes down to communication and breaking things into smaller, manageable pieces. User stories helped a lot with this because they focus on what the user actually wanted and why it matters. Looking at the requirements from the user’s perspective makes it easier to build something useful instead of just assuming what they wanted. They also help guide development by giving clear expectations for what needs to be done.

When it comes to developing programs, I’ve started to see how important it is to work in small steps instead of trying to do everything all at once. Agile practices like sprint planning, daily stand-ups, and retrospectives make it easier to stay on track and adjust when things change. Going forward, I want to keep using these kinds of processes, especially getting regular feedback and testing along the way, so I can improve my work as I go instead of waiting until the end.

To me, being a good team member in software development means being reliable, communicating clearly, and working well with others. It’s not just about finishing your own tasks, but also helping the team succeed as a whole. That means speaking up when needed, being open to feedback, and supporting your teammates. Agile really depends on teamwork, so being flexible and dependable makes a big difference.
